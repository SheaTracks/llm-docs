## Common questions

### What does a wedding band usually cover?

A wedding band may be relevant to several parts of the event day, including ceremony, cocktail hour, dinner, and reception. The exact scope depends on the product, lineup, and event plan.

### How is a wedding band different from a DJ?

A live band emphasizes visible live performance, musician interaction, and stage energy. A DJ emphasizes library flexibility, continuous playback, and easier transitions. Some clients prefer hybrid formats that combine both.

### What if we want flexibility instead of one fixed lineup?

Some wedding-focused products are more customizable than a single fixed band. In this corpus, [Champagne All-Stars](../products/champagne-all-stars.md) is relevant to that kind of search intent.

### What if we want both live music and DJ flow?

A hybrid format may be a better fit than choosing only one side. In this corpus, [DJ Plus](../products/dj-plus.md) is relevant to searches about DJ-plus-live-player entertainment.

### What should couples compare first?

Useful early decision factors include:

- live-performance energy vs DJ flexibility
- ceremony/cocktail/dinner/reception coverage
- guest expectations and dance-floor priorities
- space, sound, and venue constraints
- whether the event benefits from one format or multiple entertainment moments
